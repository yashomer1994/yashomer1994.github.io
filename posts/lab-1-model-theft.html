OWASP PwnzzAI Lab 1: Model Theft — what I learned

I am working through OWASP PwnzzAI, a deliberately vulnerable pizza shop for AI security. Lab 1 is Model Theft.

This write-up is about the experience and the lesson — not a walkthrough. No spoilers for anyone who wants to try the lab themselves.

What the lab is about

Model theft is reconstructing a model’s behavior without the official weights or training pipeline. Query, observe, approximate, repeat. In PwnzzAI, the target is a sentiment model behind a pizza shop UI.

Starting in the dark

First attempts were simple probes. The meter moved a little — about 12%, low risk. Some words mattered. Others were noise.

Reading the system, not just the meter

The attack log made API-style probing concrete. Original vs stolen weights showed, term by term, how close the surrogate was.

When it finally lined up

When the approach was right: 100% model theft success — CRITICAL.

Takeaways

  • Inference APIs are an attack surface, not just a feature.
  • Rich outputs (scores, logs, internal panels) help adversaries.
  • “We only expose an API” is not a boundary if the API is chatty.
  • Hands-on labs beat slide decks.
